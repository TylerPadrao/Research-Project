The COVID-19 impact on the remote worker’s communication and collaboration

Employee Engagement

Working from home raises the importance of the companies’ ability to communicate and engage with their employees. Clear guidelines and in-depth clarification of responsibilities are required to deal with the new ways of working and retain employee engagement (Loten, 2020).

Effective Communication

Communication should be much more frequent as we are working remotely. If someone asks a question in person, you answer it immediately. If someone asks a question over email or text, there should be an immediate or quick response, so it simulates the same concept as in person. Effective communication such as these suggestions, increases engagement and productivity (Belgium, 2020).

Employee Trust

Remote workers feel like they are part of the team and organization, and thus feel greater trust in you as their manager. Each of these elements relates to engagement and helps boost performance and productivity as well. To build trust within remote workers during COVID-19, managers must communicate what is expected of them in terms of performance level, project prioritization, and the volume of work expected. Moreover, managers must make sure remote workers have what they need and provide positive feedback (Madell, 2020).

Collaboration

Since our main communication is not face-to-face, often times there will be many misinterpretations employees have to deal with which may cause frustration and annoyance. Employees may often feel disconnected with their peers and lack collaboration. Collaboration tools such as Slack and Trello can improve morale, productivity, focus, and communication. Tools such as these, can decrease feelings of loneliness and isolation while working from home, so remote workers can instantly collaborate with their colleagues leading to a more productive workday (Hanson, 2020).
