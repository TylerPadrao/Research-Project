Home Health Technology Communication References

The COVID-19 impact on the remote worker has had both positive and negative effects on emotional and physical well-being

Work Life Balance

Employees are craving for help with work-life balance as they are working longer hours and are having a difficult time unplugging. It can result in a fusion of work and private life and some employees have a difficult time disengaging at the end of the day. Employers need to respect the private lives of their employees when they are away from their source of communication. Finding this balanced lifestyle will ensure an important balance of mental and physical health as well as maximize productivity (Eck & Belgium, 2020).

Productivity

Nearly 1/3 of newly remote workers struggle to stay productive. Many types of distractions from home such as kids, pets, and outside noise can cause this unproductivity. In a work environment, these distractions do not occur. Lack of feeling productive can lead to stress and poor mental health (Hanson, 2020).

Employee Collaboration

Some employees are having a difficult time collaborating with their peers. To combat this, companies are creatively planning off-time moments such as virtual coffee sessions, luncheons, or bring your kids to work day to help share emotions with colleagues during these difficult times (Belgium, 2020).

Flexibility

Working from home allows for more flexibility and better health and morale. If proper work life balance can be maintained, it can provide flexibility in the work day allowing workers to exercise at a time that works best for them, cook lunches that are healthier, and save money from the lack of commuting and reduction in spend on expensive business attire (Shukla, 2020).

Stress

Some studies report telecommuting reduced stress by as much as 82%. There are a few factors why home-based employment is less stressful than traditional office jobs, such as no daily commute, greater control over work environment, and improved work-life balance (Anderson, 2019).
